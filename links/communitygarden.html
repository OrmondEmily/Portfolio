Tarboro Community Garden

This is a community garden map I designed for the Tarboro Community Demonstration Garden. This was a project in collaboration with Freedom Org. The plan for this garden is to increase health equity by providing plots to grow food. The garden takes previously vacant parcels of land and turns them into flood-resistant, communal spaces to learn, grow, and harvest. The goal of this map is to help community members navigate and identify plants in the garden.

This was a self-led project where I developed strong time management and self-motivation skills. I was given an outline of the garden and transformed it in Adobe Illustrator into an aesthetically pleasing and easier to navigate map. This involved careful, precise placement of the plants to match the outline and ensure the design reflected the real layout. Inspired by landscaping icons, I created a legend with a unique icon for each plant and object in the garden.

Botanical Illustrations

I completed 6 Botanical Illustrations of NC Native plants in Procreate. I edited the final drawings in Adobe Illustrator. They are intended to be an educational resource to help community members identify NC Native plants available in their community garden.

Garden Plant Information

I documented the 38 plants available in the commmunity garden. This includes the scientific name, favorable growing conditions, nativity, edibility, and cultural and ecological significance. This document is also intended to be an educational and practical resource to help those in the community learn more about the plants in their garden.
