Applied GIS

Applying Buffers, Intersects, and Clips

I created this map in QGIS to see where bird conservation sites and wind turbine noise pollution overlap. I created an 10 mile buffer around the wind turbine locations to represent the emmited noise pollution. I then preformed an intersect to see where these areas interfered with bird conservation zones. This shows how GIS can be applied to conservation planning by analyzing potential conflicts between renewable energy development and wildlife protection.

Biverate Mapping

I created this map in ArcGIS to investigate the effect of AQI and temperature on Asthma hospitalization rates. I used a bivariate color scheme to reveal where high environmental stressors, like poor AQI and high temperatures, are associated with high asthma hospitalization rates. This shows how GIS can be applied to public health and reveal potential hotspots of health vunerability.
